SymptoTrack

App Description

Our solution takes the form of an app tailored to college students ages 18-24, providing a platform to input health symptoms before seeing their health provider. While our primary target audience consists of college students, our app offers benefits that extend to a broader user base, particularly individuals with limited income who struggle to access healthcare due to financial constraints. Users can utilize questionnaire forms within the app to track and identify symptoms, supplementing their descriptions with visual evidence through image uploads. These files are categorized by tags, facilitating easy filtering by date, type, or duration of symptoms. Users retain the flexibility to edit or delete symptom records, with a precautionary “Are you sure?” pop-up to prevent accidental deletions.

Our app addresses the challenge many college students face in accessing timely healthcare, where scheduling appointments with primary care providers can be frustratingly difficult. SymptoTracker serves as an intermediary, capturing and managing symptoms to prevent the frustration of forgetting vital health concerns during long-awaited doctor visits. Unlike mere photo documentation, our app allows users to organize symptoms with detailed descriptions, ensuring smoother interactions during medical appointments.

  • Users: The application is tailored for 18-24-year-old college students. It aims to enhance their health management by enabling them to record significant health events. The app also focuses on its impact on minority groups, providing equitable health tracking capabilities.
  • Interactive Features: Users will engage with the app through several interactive features:
    • Capability to fill out forms detailing their symptoms and uploading pictures for reference.
    • Flexibility to revisit and modify previously filled forms, with options to sort and organize them chronologically.
    • Tools to visualize symptom trends over time, such as viewing the frequency of reported symptoms per month across the year.
    • Functionality to categorize symptom records by tags for streamlined searching and sorting by symptoms, severity, and other criteria.
